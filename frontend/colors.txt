For a website that matches the glossy jelly mascot and logo, I’d lean into a bright “liquid candy” palette with strong contrast so the blue jelly really pops.

Main Colors
Deep Jelly Blue
#0A3D91
Use for: headers, nav bars, buttons, footer backgrounds

This gives the site depth and keeps it from feeling too pastel or childish.

Electric Aqua
#35D6FF
Use for: gradients, hover states, highlights, cards, interactive elements

This matches the shiny translucent feel of the mascot and logo lettering.

Accent Colors
Soft Glow Cyan
#9BF2FF
Use for: borders, glow effects, subtle backgrounds, input focus states

Adds that glossy “wet jelly” shine without overpowering the design.

Jelly Pink (optional accent)
#FF7ACF
Use sparingly for: CTA buttons, notifications, badges, tiny details

This works because the mascot already has subtle pink tones in the mouth, so it ties in naturally and gives the palette some energy.

Suggested Gradient

A signature gradient for hero sections/buttons:

#0A3D91 → #1B7BFF → #35D6FF


For this style, I’d avoid pure black and instead use a very dark navy-blue background so the jelly colors glow naturally without looking harsh.

My top recommendation:

#071426

This gives:

strong contrast with the bright aqua logo
a modern game/app feel
enough blue undertone to support the jelly aesthetic
room for glow effects and gradients without disappearing into black
